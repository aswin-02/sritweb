Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster (ACTRC)-SRIT

Cluster Description: Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster consists of investigating novel computer architectures, computer languages, systems software, performance analysis, and algorithms for next-generation HPC. The cluster is equipped with the software and hardware components which are used for research and teaching purposes.

Access to the Cluster: Access to the Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster is restricted to authorized members, such as students, faculty, and staff of SRIT. Access may also be granted to outside researchers and visiting faculty, subject to the approval of the cluster head.

Members Responsibility: Members are responsible for observing all policies and guidelines regarding the use of the facilities in the Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster. Members should take all necessary precautions to protect the confidentiality and integrity of their data.

Data Handling and Management: All data stored must be backed up regularly. Members should also take responsibility for the retention and archiving of their data, and should ensure that they have adequate backup copies.

Software Installation: Installation of new software packages on the Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster is strictly controlled by the cluster head. Members who need to install new software must first obtain approval from the cluster head and provide a detailed description of the software and its intended use.

Members Support: The cluster head is responsible for providing technical support to members of the Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster. Members are encouraged to report any problems they encounter, and to seek help when they need it.

Violations of Policy: Any violations of the policies and procedures outlined in this SOP may result in the suspension or revocation of a member's access to the Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster. The cluster administrator will investigate all reported violations, and will take appropriate action, in accordance with College policies and procedures.

Review and Revision of SOP: The SOP for the Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster will be reviewed and revised periodically to reflect changes in technology, policies, and best practices. The cluster head is responsible for ensuring that this SOP remains up-to- date and relevant to the cluster.

  • The research cluster is intended to provide support for teaching members' as well as students ongoing research projects.
  • To provide opportunities for cross-disciplinary research collaboration;
  • To act as forums for structured exchange of theories and methodologies across disciplines of engineering and new technologies.
  • The vision of Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster is to be at the forefront of innovation in the field of computer science and technology and make innovative computing technologies that solve real-world.
  • To develop new algorithms, architectures, and software systems that can be used in a variety of applications, such as scientific simulation, big data analytics, andmedical diagnosis, among others.
  • To be a catalyst for innovation and progress in the field of computer science and technology.
  • The mission of Advanced Computing Technologies Research Cluster is to conduct cutting-edge research in the field of computer science and technology.
  • To develop new and innovative solutions to real-world problems that includes areas such as high-performance computing, artificial intelligence, machinelearning, computer architecture, and software engineering, among others.
  • To discover solutions that have a significant impact on society, education and outreach activities, including working with universities, research institutions, and industry partners to promote the development of new technologies
